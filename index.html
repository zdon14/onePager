Struggling Teams

Dive into what teams are looking to get their season back on track

As we are over a month into the NHL season, one team that has been a huge let down is the St. Louis Blues. After having a great season last year, making it to the quarter finals where they lost to the future Stanley Cup champions in the Colorado Avalanche, their season so far has been nothing short of a let down. The Blues are struggling to generate offense, and as of 11/3 , their record sits at an underwhelming 3-6. One other struggling team that we'll dive into is the Pittsburgh Penguins. Like the blues, the Penguins had a good season last year, where they lost in the first round of the playoffs to a very good New York Rangers team. The Penguins were able to keep their core together, and looking to run it back for one more season, but so far it has been very underwhelming. With this likely being the last year that the Penguins will have Crosby, Malkin, and Letang all playing together, the start to their season has been very disappointing. As of 11/3 , the Penguins record sits at 4-6-2 .

The Top Dog

Take a Look Into how the Bruins Have Found Success early on this year

The Bruins are off to a hot start, as their record as of 11/3 sits at an almost perfect 9-1. The Bruins started off the season missing three of their top players, in Charlie Mcavoy, Matt Grezylck, and Brad Marchand. However, the Bruins have battled through adversity, and proved everyone wrong. Rather than hardly being able to get by, like people anticipated, the Bruins have thrived in the early part of this season, and sit at the top of the power rankings. One of the key reasons for the Bruins success is their goaltending: the B's have had remarkable goaltending between Linus Ullmark and Jeremy Swayman. With Charlie Mcavoy soon to come back, the Bruins look to keep up the success.
